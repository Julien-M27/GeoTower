# 2. Purpose of the application

GeoTower lets you view **mobile telephony sites and antennas** in France on a map, based on **open public data** (in particular ANFR, ARCEP, data.gouv.fr), together with map backgrounds and elevation data (IGN, OpenStreetMap).

The information shown is provided **for guidance only**. It comes from third-party sources and may contain errors, update delays or inaccuracies. GeoTower does not guarantee that it is accurate or complete.

# 3. Personal data and privacy

GeoTower is designed around the principle of **data minimisation**.

**GeoTower creates no account, requires no registration and collects no identity data** (no name, no e-mail, no phone number, no advertising identifier).

## 3.1 No collection by the publisher

The publisher of GeoTower receives, stores and records **no data** about you:
- no usage logs;
- no statistics or analytics tools;
- no advertising trackers;
- no telemetry.

No usage data leaves your device to reach the publisher.

## 3.2 Location

With your permission, GeoTower uses your device's location to centre the map and show nearby sites, as well as for the **real-time tracking** ("live") feature, which may require **background** location access in order to continuously identify the mobile site you are potentially connected to.

Your location is **processed locally, on your device**. It is **never sent to the publisher** or to any third party for tracking purposes. You can withdraw the location permission at any time in the Android settings; the relevant features will then be disabled.

## 3.3 Data stored on your device

GeoTower only stores, in its private space on your device: your **preferences**, the downloaded **database** of sites, and the **history of your photo uploads** (see section 4). This data stays on the device, is not backed up to the cloud, and is removed when you uninstall the application.

# 4. Community photos (upload to SignalQuest)

GeoTower lets you, **if you wish**, upload one or more **photos of a site** (which you choose from your gallery or take with the camera) in order to enrich the **SignalQuest** community database, an independent third-party service.

## 4.1 What is sent

When you confirm an upload, the following is sent to SignalQuest:
- the **photo** (resized and recompressed);
- the **description** you enter freely (optional);
- the **operator** and the **identification codes** of the site concerned (e.g. ANFR code);
- where applicable, the photo's **EXIF metadata**.

> EXIF metadata: a photo may contain technical information (date, device model) and, where applicable, the **GPS location** where it was taken. Before each upload, GeoTower offers to remove this metadata. **This option is disabled by default**: if you do not enable it, the EXIF metadata is sent with the photo. Remember to enable it if you do not wish to share this information.

The upload is **anonymous**: no data identifying you is attached to the photo (no name, no account, no identifier).

## 4.2 Once the photo has been sent

As soon as a photo is uploaded, it is **hosted and managed exclusively by SignalQuest**. **GeoTower no longer has access to it, keeps no remote copy of it and has no control over it.**

All photos uploaded from GeoTower are published on SignalQuest **under the collective name "GeoTower"**, and not under an individual account — unlike users who have their own SignalQuest account. There is therefore **no faster way** to specifically locate your uploads.

To have a photo removed, you must:
1. go to SignalQuest (app or website), in the **Map** tab;
2. find the photo concerned;
3. **report** it, asking for its removal.

You may also contact SignalQuest at **support@signalquest.fr**. GeoTower cannot delete or modify on your behalf a photo hosted by SignalQuest.

## 4.3 Local history

GeoTower keeps, **on your device only**, a history of your uploads (thumbnails and status), which you can clear at any time from the "Upload history" screen. Clearing this local history does not remove photos already published on SignalQuest.
